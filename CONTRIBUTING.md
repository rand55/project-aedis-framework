# Contributing to project-aedis-framework

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Advanced Economic Development and Infrastructure System (AEDIS). This framework is designed to address AI-driven workforce displacement by transitioning global economies toward structural, asset-backed infrastructure expansion. 

Because no single entity can account for the unique legal, cultural, and material constraints of 195 nations, this project relies heavily on community collaboration to build out **Regional Annexes** while safeguarding the **Universal Core**.

---

## The Code of Engagement

### 1. Focus on Empirical Improvement
We welcome rigorous, data-driven criticism, alternative economic modeling, and edge-case testing. However, all contributions must focus on refining or adapting the framework to meet real-world realities rather than defending legacy power structures that rely on artificial scarcity.

### 2. The Humanitarian Baseline
AEDIS maintains a strict, non-negotiable prohibition against funding military operations, combat systems, or destructive technologies. Contributions or extensions that attempt to bypass or weaken this firewall will be rejected immediately.

### 3. AI-Assisted Development
We actively encourage contributors to use advanced AI models to analyze the framework, stress-test calculations, and generate localized legal language. When submitting AI-generated annexes or translations, please verify the output for accuracy and legal validity before submitting a Pull Request.

---

## How Can I Contribute?

### Develop Regional Annexes
The Universal Core provides the economic engine, but we need customized legal structures to implement it globally. You can help by drafting Annex templates for specific legal traditions:
* **Common Law Systems** (e.g., balancing SIC issuance with legislative appropriations and debt limits)
* **Civil Law Systems** (e.g., rewriting administrative and procurement codes for milestone-based clearing)
* **Sharia-Compliant Systems** (e.g., aligning asset-backed infrastructure creation with Islamic finance principles)
* **Customary or Localized Frameworks**

### Economic Simulation & Stress Testing
Help us run data models on the automated safeguards:
* Testing the responsiveness of the **Material Constraint Oracle** to commodity spikes.
* Simulating capital velocity and currency stabilization during a **Dynamic Threshold Scaling** sequence.
* Stress-testing the **SIC Sink** threshold metrics.

### Global Translations
To truly empower a global popular mandate, the core document must be accessible in every language. Help us translate the `README.md` and `AEDIS_MASTER_PROPOSAL.md`. AI-translated drafts are highly welcome as a baseline, but require native-speaker review for nuance.

## Discussion Categories & Issue Tracking

When opening an issue or starting a discussion thread, please categorize your contribution under one of the following domains to keep workflows organized:

* **`[Core-Economics]`** — Discussions regarding Sovereign Infrastructure Credit, inflation safeguards, or capital mechanics.
* **`[Legal-Annex]`** — Drafts, challenges, or precedents related to regional or constitutional integration.
* **`[Tech-Ledger]`** — Technical specifications for the federated blockchain public ledger, mesh network logistics, or zero-knowledge proof voting systems.
* **`[Phase-Zero]`** — Resource mapping, material supply chains, or industrial training program structures.
* **`[Translation]`** — Coordination for multilingual localization efforts.

---

## Pull Request Process

1. **Check Existing Work:** Ensure no one else is currently working on the same translation or legal annex by searching active Issues and Pull Requests.
2. **Isolate Your Changes:** Keep PRs highly targeted (e.g., adding a single language translation or a specific regional legal template). Do not mix multiple unrelated updates.
3. **Documentation:** Ensure any modifications or architectural additions are fully documented following the layout established in `AEDIS_MASTER_PROPOSAL.md`.
4. **Community Review:** All submissions will undergo peer review by the community to ensure adherence to the Universal Core and Humanitarian Baseline before merging.
